CS50 is Harvard University's introduction to the intellectual enterprises of computer science and the art of programming, CS50 AP is a free adaptation of CS50 for high schools, and CS50 AP Puyo is a modified version of CS50 AP which is taught in a city located in the Amazon Region of Ecuador called Puyo.

This is a beta project which goal is to show the importance of teaching coding skills to high school students. The classes are taught in a high school called San Vicente Ferrer because this was the high school where the instructor studied, so the teachers, who already know him, supported his idea of teaching a beta programming course, and the principal allowed him to use the high school's facilities completely for free.
